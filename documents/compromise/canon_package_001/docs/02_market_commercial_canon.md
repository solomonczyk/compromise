# Market and Commercial Canon

## Market frame
The product enters the online recruitment market. Mature competitors prove demand, but they also create user fatigue: mass applications, recruiter spam, fake posts, difficult differentiation and expensive employer tools. Compromise starts as a narrow marketplace with guided trust rather than a broad database.

## Competitor classes
### Large global networks
LinkedIn and Indeed own reach, network effects and recruiter tooling. They are not beatable by size. They are relevant because employers already understand paid job posts, sponsored reach, recruiter search and AI-assisted sourcing.

### Regional and local job boards
In Serbia and neighboring markets, local boards such as Infostud/Poslovi and IT-focused boards such as HelloWorld are strong references. The product should not copy their full breadth. It should start with a narrower wedge: moderated multilingual hiring and guided employer support.

### HH.ru-like model
HH.ru-style products monetize employer access: job posting, visibility, resume search, employer tools and recruitment workflow. Compromise adopts the lesson that employers pay, but rejects early resume database monetization because trust and liquidity are not ready.

### Public employment networks
EURES and public employment services show that cross-border employment information and vacancy matching exist as public infrastructure. Compromise should not promise legal mobility advice unless reviewed.

## Primary segment
The first segment is small and medium employers hiring operational, service, support, sales, junior digital or multilingual roles where personal trust and speed matter more than enterprise ATS features. The candidate side focuses on people willing to complete a structured profile and apply through a safer flow.

## First sellable result
A paid pilot for one employer: create or improve one vacancy, publish it after moderation, collect structured applications, optionally help shortlist candidates manually, and provide a concise outcome report. This is the smallest honest paid result.

## Commercial model
MVP commercial model:
- Candidate core access: free.
- Employer free boundary: create account, draft vacancy, preview moderation requirements.
- Employer paid boundary: publish a moderated vacancy with support and application handling.
- Pilot price hypothesis: 49 EUR entry, 99 EUR standard, 149 EUR with concierge shortlisting.
- Payment method at first: manual invoice, bank transfer or payment link. Automated recurring billing is outside MVP.

## Pricing hypotheses
1. Employers will pay 49 EUR for one moderated vacancy if they believe candidate quality is higher than free posting channels.
2. Employers will pay 99 EUR if the platform reduces manual sorting through structured applications and basic support.
3. Employers will pay 149 EUR if the founder/operator provides concierge shortlisting for a hard-to-fill role.
4. Candidates will not pay in the MVP because payment would reduce supply and harm marketplace liquidity.

## Unit economics
Initial unit model per paid employer:
- Revenue per paid pilot: 49–149 EUR.
- Manual support target: less than 2.5 hours per paid vacancy.
- Infrastructure target: less than 20 EUR/month during beta.
- AI API target: less than 30 EUR per stage unless ADR approves more.
- Gross margin target before scale: at least 40 percent after direct support cost estimate.

## Willingness-to-pay validation
Valid evidence is actual payment, signed pilot approval with scheduled payment, or a written buying commitment from a qualified employer after price is shown. Invalid evidence is praise, likes, vague interest, or candidate-only traffic.

## First form of sale
Direct founder-led sale. The founder contacts target employers, offers a bounded paid pilot, explains that the product is early, and avoids claims about guaranteed hires. The sale script must describe deliverables, timebox, refund condition and privacy handling.

## Free and paid boundary
Free: browse public vacancies, create candidate profile, apply, create employer account, draft vacancy. Paid: publish employer vacancy after moderation, receive candidate applications in employer workspace, receive support and pilot report.

## Marketing promise limitations
Allowed claims: moderated vacancies, structured applications, safer hiring flow, early pilot support. Forbidden claims: guaranteed hiring, best candidates in market, automated unbiased AI decisions, legal migration advice, production-scale reliability before S8.
