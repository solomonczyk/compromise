# Product Canon

## Product definition
Compromise is a moderated hiring marketplace connecting employers and candidates through structured vacancies, structured candidate profiles, applications, and trust controls. It is not a general social network, not a pure parser, not a staffing agency and not an uncontrolled job aggregator.

## Problem
Small employers and early teams struggle to hire because broad job boards create three failures: noisy applications, weak trust and high operational effort. Candidates also face fake postings, unclear requirements, ghosting, and repetitive profile submission. A useful product must reduce uncertainty for both sides, not merely publish more vacancies.

## Primary customer
The primary paying customer is a small or medium employer that has at least one active hiring need, lacks a mature recruiting department, and needs a credible candidate pipeline quickly. This is a provisional decision because payment evidence is still required in S6.

## Primary users
Candidates use the platform without paying in the MVP. Their core need is safe discovery of real vacancies, simple application, status clarity and control over personal data. Admin/moderator is a first-class internal user because trust is the MVP differentiator.

## Jobs-to-be-Done
1. When an employer needs to hire without a recruiter, they want to publish a clear vacancy and receive relevant applications without being flooded by irrelevant profiles.
2. When a candidate is looking for work, they want to understand whether a job is real, whether they match, and what happened after applying.
3. When a moderator reviews marketplace content, they want to identify scam, low-quality or incomplete postings before they harm trust.
4. When the founder sells the first pilot, they want a bounded paid promise that can be delivered manually if automation is incomplete.

## Personas
### Employer Elena — small business owner
Elena hires operational, support, sales or service roles. She has no HR department. She values speed, clarity and safety. She pays only if the result feels concrete: a moderated vacancy, visible candidates or guided shortlisting.

### Recruiter Marko — internal recruiter in a growing team
Marko needs structured applications and less manual screening. He will not migrate to a new platform unless the first workflow is simpler than spreadsheets and messages.

### Candidate Andrii — multilingual job seeker
Andrii wants real vacancies, clear language requirements, safe communication and deletion/export rights. He dislikes platforms that expose too much data or never answer applications.

### Moderator Ana — trust operator
Ana reviews employer identity, vacancy clarity, suspicious contact patterns and candidate reports. She needs audit logs, status reasons and safe escalation paths.

## Value proposition
Compromise offers a trusted and guided hiring loop: employers publish moderated vacancies and receive structured applications; candidates apply safely; moderators protect marketplace quality. The first paid value is not unlimited access. The first paid value is a controlled hiring attempt with reduced noise.

## Positioning
Compromise is positioned as a focused, trust-first hiring marketplace for early employer demand, starting narrower than large platforms. It competes on moderation, clarity, candidate safety and practical guided hiring rather than size of database.

## Competitive differences
- Moderated vacancies before public trust is requested.
- Candidate status clarity as a product requirement, not an afterthought.
- Employer-paid pilot tied to a specific vacancy and support result.
- AI is advisory and audited, not a hidden authority.
- Growth is blocked until payment and support evidence exist.

## Product principles
1. Trust beats volume during the first market phase.
2. Every public promise must map to a shipped and tested capability.
3. Candidates do not pay for core access in MVP.
4. Employers pay for a bounded hiring result, not vague platform potential.
5. AI suggestions require human accountability.
6. Technical success does not imply business, visual or production acceptance.
7. Privacy controls are part of MVP, not a later legal polish.

## Non-goals
- No attempt to beat LinkedIn, Indeed or HH.ru on database size in MVP.
- No automated rejection or ranking as authoritative decision.
- No paid resume database access in MVP.
- No mobile native app in MVP.
- No self-serve subscription billing before paid pilot evidence.
- No international tax, payroll, contract or visa automation in MVP.
- No guarantee that a vacancy will be filled.

## MVP scope
The MVP contains candidate account, employer account, profile basics, job posting, application, employer application view, admin moderation, basic notifications, audit logs, analytics events, support process, deletion and export.

## Hard MVP cut line
Anything not required for one employer to pay for one moderated hiring attempt is outside MVP. Search relevance can be simple. Design can be clean but minimal. AI can assist with writing and classification, but cannot decide hiring outcomes.

## Product Definition of Done
The product layer is done only when: the core hiring loop works end-to-end; every state has owner and recovery; security isolation is proven; support can answer real users; analytics measures the funnel; one paid pilot can be sold and delivered; production remains closed until S8 operator acceptance.
