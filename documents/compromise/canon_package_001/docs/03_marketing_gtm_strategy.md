# Marketing and Go-To-Market Strategy

## GTM principle
Marketing is not allowed to run ahead of product readiness. A channel can open only when the product stage proves the promised capability. Paid acquisition is blocked until S6 paid pilot evidence exists.

## Channels
1. Direct outreach to employers: founder-led messages, local business groups, LinkedIn, chambers, small agencies.
2. Candidate supply: Telegram/Facebook groups, local communities, content posts, referral invitations.
3. Content: practical hiring and job-search guides, scam warning posts, vacancy quality checklists.
4. Partnerships: language schools, relocation communities, local service businesses, small HR consultants.
5. Paid ads: small controlled test only in S7 after paid pilot.

## Content strategy
Content must solve concrete friction: how to write a clear vacancy, how candidates avoid fake jobs, how employers reduce irrelevant applications, how multilingual hiring works, how application status should be handled. Content must not claim market leadership.

## Funnel
Employer funnel: landing visit → employer lead form → qualification call/message → draft vacancy → moderation → paid pilot offer → payment → published vacancy → applications → pilot report → renewal/referral.

Candidate funnel: landing visit → vacancy browse → account creation → profile completion → apply → status update → feedback/request → referral.

## Landing requirements
The landing page must state: who the platform is for, the first result, free/paid boundary, early-stage status, privacy controls, support contact, and a simple employer lead form. It must not display fake metrics.

## Onboarding
Employer onboarding asks for company identity, hiring need, vacancy details, location/remote mode, language requirements, compensation range or reason for hiding it, contact method, and consent to moderation. Candidate onboarding asks for profile basics, experience, languages, location/remote preferences, contact preference and consent.

## Trial and beta
A free trial is limited to draft vacancy and preview. Closed beta allows real postings only by invitation and moderation. Beta users receive clear early-stage terms and support expectations.

## Paid pilot
Paid pilot starts when an employer pays for a bounded vacancy campaign. The product must capture payment evidence and delivery evidence separately. Payment alone is not product success if delivery fails.

## Referral
Referral starts after a satisfied employer or candidate completes a real flow. Referral reward is non-monetary in MVP: priority review, profile badge after human approval, or extra support. Monetary rewards are blocked until reward/money gate is accepted.

## KPI per transition
- Landing to employer lead: minimum 3 percent before ads, 5 percent for S7 channel test.
- Employer lead to qualified call/message: minimum 30 percent.
- Qualified employer to draft vacancy: minimum 40 percent.
- Draft vacancy to paid pilot: minimum 20 percent during direct sale test.
- Published vacancy to first application: within 7 days during beta.
- Active job applications: minimum 3 per active job in S5.
- Paid pilot refund rate: maximum 50 percent in S6, then lower before scale.

## Criteria to launch advertising
Paid ads require S6 GO, no open P0/P1 UX blockers, landing conversion tracking, reviewed ad claims, support capacity, spending cap, and channel stop rule. Without those, ads remain closed.

## Stop rules for channels
Stop a channel if it exceeds budget cap without qualified employer leads, attracts only candidates when employer demand is required, creates misleading expectations, overwhelms support, or produces abuse reports above safety threshold.
