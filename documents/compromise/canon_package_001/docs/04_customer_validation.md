# Customer Validation Canon

## Validation sequence
The project validates demand in this order: problem interviews, message test, landing test, waitlist, concierge prototype, usability test, candidate alpha, closed beta, paid pilot. Later evidence cannot erase earlier safety blockers.

## Problem interviews
Minimum S4 sample: 10 qualified employers and 10 candidates. A qualified employer currently has or recently had a hiring need. A qualified candidate is actively or recently looking for work. Interview notes must capture current process, pain, workaround, cost, urgency and buying authority.

## Message test
Test three concise messages: trust-first hiring, faster relevant applications, guided vacancy pilot. Valid signal is respondent explaining the value correctly and asking a practical next step. Invalid signal is polite praise without comprehension.

## Landing test
Landing test measures source, visit, employer lead, candidate signup, form completion and privacy-page view. Fake counters and untracked campaigns are forbidden.

## Waitlist
Waitlist separates employer and candidate intent. Employer waitlist requires role to hire, timeline and contact permission. Candidate waitlist requires desired role, location/remote preference and consent.

## Concierge prototype
Before full automation, the founder can manually help one employer clarify a vacancy and manually review candidate fit. The manual work must be logged to learn what should be productized.

## Usability test
Usability tests cover candidate signup, profile completion, job search, application, employer vacancy creation, application review and admin moderation. Valid usability evidence includes screen recording or observation notes, device, browser and task outcome.

## Candidate alpha
Candidate alpha verifies profile completion and application clarity, not commercial demand. Candidate enthusiasm alone is insufficient to advance to paid pilot.

## Closed beta
Closed beta tests the marketplace loop with real users and support. The beta must stay bounded: 5 employers, 50 candidates, 20 moderated vacancies unless a change request expands it.

## Paid pilot
Paid pilot is the commercial proof gate. A real employer must pay or provide documented payment commitment with clear scope. The pilot is invalid if payment is symbolic, from a friend without hiring need, or based on misleading promises.

## Valid evidence
Valid evidence is dated, attributable, connected to a stage and independently reviewable. It includes interview notes, consent records, analytics export, support logs, payment receipt, moderation logs, screenshots and test reports.

## Invalid evidence
Invalid evidence includes unverified opinions, vanity traffic, fake seed data presented as users, technical tests used as market proof, candidate-only growth used as employer demand proof, and any result collected without privacy consent.

## GO / HOLD / PIVOT / STOP
GO means evidence meets stage thresholds and blockers are closed. HOLD means evidence is promising but incomplete. PIVOT means evidence points to a different segment, pricing or delivery model. STOP means the problem, trust or commercial path is not viable within budget.
